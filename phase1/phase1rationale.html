Phase 1 Rationale

Social and Technical Systems

Home automation is the intersection of social and technical systems. It strives to accomplish the same general goal that Facebook does: o change the way you collaborate and connect with others. Instead of other human beings, home automation changes the way you collaborate with your home, as well as how items in your home interact with one another. Similar to how before Facebook you had to have a physical interaction with other individuals in your life, your interaction with physical objects in your home has turned virtual. The home automation system itself will potentially interface with the home’s utilities (water, electricity, phone/internet) as well as individual appliances and heating/cooling systems. Therefore, the design should minimize the possibility for damage to these and not increase the likelihood of a safety hazard.

Design Implications

Users: As mentioned above in our initial success criteria, our user’s economic and social factors will bring an influence to our design. Their known technical abilities will allow us to add more functionality into the system without the fear of it becoming too advanced. Their economic awareness and concerns will force our design to push beyond the standard set of home automation features and incorporate money savings rather than just time savings.

Environment: Our design must be sensitive to a user’s connection with the objects/ tasks we are considering in the home automation space. While some tasks such as ‘watering the plants’ or ‘making coffee’ are great examples for incorporating into a home automation design, it’s important to remember that these may be enjoyable task for users to complete, when time allows. Home automation should allow users to continue to perform tasks manually, without disturbing the automation schedule.

Design Space Justification

Justification for this effort is to build upon the modern-day innovations we see every day. We live in a world driven by “convenience.” Technology is creating positive disruptions within everyday industries that haven’t seen change in decades. These disruptions are made up of the Internet of Things (IoT) and should not be limited; therefore, exploring the opportunities and possibilities available within in-home automation is just the beginning in bringing the benefits of technology to everyone, all around the world. To begin, we must understand how tasks are performed today, the pain points and frustrations. Only then, can we expose opportunities to be leveraged by technology.
