Appendix 1

Interview Questions/Survey

The following section documents various interviews used gather learning and information to support the design.

Interview #1

1.     (Question) How can IoT or smart home experiences improve your life?

a.     I can have better insights into the consumption of utilities to influence cost of living expenses.

b.     Smart home technologies help me in managing situations for elderly or disabled care for loved one’s.

c.     I am a frequent traveler and smart home technologies enable the ability to monitor and manage my devices in home from anywhere; therefore, providing proper security and assurance my belongings are safe while away.

d.     Smart home technologies just make my life easier, more convenient, and comfortable.

2.     (Question) What are common tasks that would be most beneficial from a smart home?

a.     Ability to monitor the consumption of utilities such as water, electricity, and air-conditioning.

b.     Security and monitoring of home (perimeter, individual rooms, garage, primary and secondary walkways, and doors/windows).

c.     Ability to control devices by voice or by mobile (lights, entertainment, etc.).

d.     Ability to create common routines to drive themes or events. (movies, good-morning, or good-night, etc.)

3.     (Question) What are the common areas, most important to one’s home?

a.     Utilities

b.     Home Security

c.     Home Health

d.     Entertainment

4.     (Question) What are the negative concerns around home automation or a smart home?

a.     Solutions are complex and I would increase monthly expenses.

b.     Security and privacy.

c.     Longevity of technology? Are there support lifecycles around the IoT devices and services?

d.     Is the technology proprietary; therefore, limiting me to a single vendor or manufacturer selection.

e.     Large upfront cost that result in the possibility of only small monthly savings.

5.     (Question) What benefits do smart home technologies provide beyond an individual person?

a.     I would like to manage the utilities, health, and security for rental or vacation properties.

b.     I would like to receive notifications if a window or door is opened while not at home. Additionally, I would like a single dashboard that tells me which window or door is opened (or the state of a device).

c.     I would like to closer insights in consumption spending for utilities.

Interview #2

Note: It should be noted that both individuals interviewed do not pay utility bills for their apartment homes.

Female, 37 years old

I am interested in home automation.

somewhat disagree

I am interested in 'smart' appliances

strongly disagree

I have used a home automation system...

never

Name home appliances/tasks that would benefit from automation.

vacuuming, robot vacuum

self-cleaning bathrooms

What home appliances/tasks would you like to be able to control remotely (from, say, a smartphone or tablet while away from home)?  These do not need to be the ones listed above.

kiln - (specialty appliance)

oven... worried about safety checking on something left cooking while away

checking for lights on/off (would like to be able to turn off lights that have been left on)

shut-off outlets to conserve electricity

This subject had general concerns about smart appliances as it relates to personal privacy. She was concerned both about the possibility that such services or devices could be hacked by others as well as the ability for companies to eavesdrop on her habits and learn too much about her and her family.

Other comments

"Automation is lazy."

"Maybe we need to have different priorities" if we need to both have the appliances and then another system to manage them all.

But then again, "What if I am too sick?"  Home automation could allow me to function normally even when sick or needing help for other reasons.

Male, 43 years old

I am interested in home automation.

agree somewhat

I am interested in 'smart' appliances

neutral

"I am concerned about my personal privacy and the ability of 'smart' devices to spy on me."

I have used a home automation system...

Never

Name home appliances/tasks that would benefit from automation.

kitchen appliances (oven/stove top)

laundry

What home appliances/tasks would you like to be able to control remotely (from, say, a smartphone or tablet while away from home)?  These do not need to be the ones listed above.

oven/stove (check temp, "did I leave it on?

door locks (not for unlocking, again, just to check up)

pantry/fridge check on what I have

Other Comments

This individual also had personal privacy concerns and referenced news reports about hacked smart devices being used in cyber-attacks.  He is intrigued by the possibilities offered by home automation but does not feel that the technology itself is mature enough.  “It could provide peace of mind” in a did-I-leave-the-stove-on situation.

Male, 27 years old

agree

strongly agree

If you indicated 'yes' in the last question, please describe your experience.  What was good/bad?  Why?

"I have a iRobot vacuum, it works okay, although I end up having to vacuum areas manually anyways. It helps control the hair from my cat, and reduces the number of times I have to use the traditional vacuum."

Name home appliances/tasks that would benefit from automation.

Other comments

"most home automation products cost a lot of money, and require a long payback period of saving money over the original investment."

I asked the subject about smart thermostats, since he had mentioned those tasks

"My apartment has a single zoned central forced air A/C heating system. The Nest [https://nest.com/thermostat/meet-nest-thermostat] would be great, but my second floor gets much hotter than the first and I would not want to buy two just to control the thermostat downstairs"

"I would want to be able to visualize the savings in the long term to make the initial investment"
