Phase 2 Rationale

Background and Process

Our process followed roughly the same flow as in phase 1. Since we each have often significantly different daily schedules and reside in different time zones, asynchronous communication via the group discussion board proved the best means of keeping up with each other's progress. We used Google Docs as our collaboration tool for authoring and editing documents and prototypes. Though there have been challenges with asynchronous communication, and some tasks are easier to conduct in this fashion than others, it is our sense that the group has been able to successfully collaborate in this way.

Brainstorming

Keeping with our plan of creating an interface to use in the home automation space, we first brainstormed several possible directions to go and several processes that a home automation interface could improve. From this session, we produced our original list of 25 design ideas. Each member of After compiling the list of 25, we discussed and edited the entries.

Refinement

Following this we sketched up each of the 25. The initial sketches provided additional insight. For example, upon taking time to physically sketch out some of the ideas, we realized that several of them overlapped significantly in scope making them inappropriate for a more fully realized draft prototype. Other ideas were rejected as possibilities for further prototyping as the operations themselves were relatively trivial, involving very little interaction. We wanted to flesh out ideas that could realize our success criteria to a greater extent. Our discussion that followed yielded the three design ideas for which we would create the rough prototypes. These were deemed to be most likely to fulfill our goals.
