The following are low-fidelity prototypes deemed right for prototyping.

Energy bill savings

Overview:
Integrate energy bill to determine cost per activity. Provide suggestions for money savings (set temperature higher/cooler, reduce shower duration by x minutes). Display money saved via various activities (lights off while away, smart thermostat, etc.) .

Prototyping ideas:
Since there will be a lot of data being presented to the user for this design aspect, I recommend an interactive powerpoint or pdf with hyperlinks to emulate functionality. This will allow graphical representations of the data - rich content which will be displayed to the user Include link back to home page Show graphs and charts about energy usages for prior month average. Compare to current usage. Means to sync with utility bill or to photograph it or upload

View Energy Prototype


Customize AM/PM routine

Overview:
Create time/action pairs for when to make adjustments to lights/temp/appliances/etc. Use familiar, scrolling “time-wheel” idiom for time setting. Allow different settings for weekday versus weekend days. The routines should be able to be suspended for time away on business trip or vacation.

Prototyping ideas:
Include link back to home page Interaction at the “full-routine” level and at the “action” level Add/Edit/Delete action Warnings for conflicting/unsafe/contradictory actions Lo-fi prototype could be a “serial” ppt or flow chart Show a timeline view for when actions occur in a routine

View Routine Prototype


Turning ceiling fans on/off

Overview:
Ability to control ceiling fans electronically or through mobile interfaces. Fans will display as animated when on and still when off.

Prototyping ideas:
Include link back to home page Provide both a “blueprint” and list-style views for modifying fan status Show fan icon spinning when fans is on
