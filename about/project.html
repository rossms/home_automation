INFO 611 Design Project

Overview: An important goal of this class is practicing design thinking. Toward this end, project teams will identify an interesting problem space and will design and prototype novel system ideas using different approaches and tools.
The deliverables for each phase of this project will be posted on an attractive project website that presents/showcases your designs and describes your design rationale and process. This website is a portfolio piece and an exercise in design, so presentation counts.

Introduction

As modern-day technologies continue to evolve, present and future generations will continue to enjoy the benefits that make our lives easier, more convenient, and more comfortable. We live in a world driven by “convenience” and made up of the Internet of Things (IoT). To summarize, IoT is where any device with an on and off switch can be connected to the Internet and/or interconnected with each other creating a giant network of connected “things” that enable relationships between people-people, people-things, and things-things. [Morgan, 2014]

Team 1 will investigate the design space for using data and technology solutions based on IoT devices to maximize the possibilities of a “smart home.” This research will provide an analysis and gather requirements to support the design activities intending to maximize the benefits of home automation by integrating common consumer appliances, entertainment systems, and home décor into a centralized interface that provides real-time analytics and a more efficient way for economic management. The results of this effort will provide a number benefits to the average middle-class consumer, that has Internet connectivity located in any metropolitan cities within the United States. The design scope of this effort will focus on four areas of the home.

  • Entertainment: Internet connected entertainment devices such as televisions, radios, gaming systems, and computers.
  • Utility Management: Monitoring of usage to determine consumption of utilities such as electric/power from devices such as control actuators, AC/heating controls, kitchen and home appliances (stove-top appliances, laundry machines, etc.) or water bills from devices such as sprinklers, faucets, etc.
  • Device Monitoring: Ability to monitor the state of electronic or non-electronic device such as windows or doors using home-environment sensors and activity controls.
  • Home Security: Monitoring through security cameras and motion sensors of perimeter and security zones of the home.

Problem Space

There is no shortage of possibilities in home automation or the ability to create a “smart home” within the evolution of IoT. By enabling devices with the intelligence to monitor and manage devices, data can be collected to programmatically drive decisions for automation. At the same time, this data can also be used to influence weekly, monthly, and annual spending trends or even used to alert authorities in case of an emergency. The use cases are limitless. Due to the lack of connectivity in many devices within the home, the ability to extract digital telemetry is not achievable; therefore, making it impossible to determine real-time state or consumption status for financial budgeting purposes, let alone manage a device from any remote computer.
